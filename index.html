Cheese for beginners: don't settle for Cheese Whiz

Cheese is an addiction. Cheddar. Mozzarella. Parmesan. Gouda. Burgers. Pizza. Spaghetti. Grilled cheese. With such a huge variety in textures and tastes, and its endless flexibility with dishes, cheese is the king ingredient that shines regardless of its place as the star or side of the dish. But. For the newbie, starting out in the world of fromage, the endless universe of cheese can be well, scary. So choose your cheese, feed your addiction, and learn on website: Cheese for Newbies. Don’t settle for Cheez Whiz, be a Cheese whiz. Be classy and upgrade from a newbie that stuffs their faces with random cheesie goodness (even though it tastes great!) to an expert that knows all about the cheese they put in their mouths. Cheese your addiction.

What is cheese?

It's is food made from processed milk curds. It can be made from any type of milk, from cow, to sheep, to goat. There’s even vegan varietes (though nutrition will differ from non-vegan cheese). In all there are probably thousands of types of cheese, ranging in flavour from sweet to sharp, in texture from soft to hard.

Health Benefits

With the huge variety of cheese to try, it’s unsurprising that the opinion on cheese is divided. Yes, it can be high in saturated fats and sodium. (Unhealthy!) Yes, you can choose varieties that are low fat, and low sodium (healthy!).But, despite this division because of fat and sodium content, cheese does contain a good amount of nutrients to our body’s benefit.

Nutrients

  • Calcium: Calcium is good for building and maintaining your body’s bones.
  • Protein: Protein is necessary in muscles maintenance and building.
  • Magnesium: Great for bones and heart, magnesium is considered one of the body’s seven essential macronutrients.
  • Zinc: Supports your immune system.
  • Vitamin A: Helps with your immune system, vision, and your growth and development.
  • Vitamin D: Helps with bone and teeth development, and lowered risk of despression
  • Vitamin K: Aids with blood clotting to prevent excess bleeding.
