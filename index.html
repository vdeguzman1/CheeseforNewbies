Cheese is an addiction. Cheddar. Mozzarella. Parmesan. Gouda. Burgers. Pizza. Spaghetti. Grilled cheese. Cheese your addiction.

What is cheese?

The main ingredient in this is milk. From cow, to sheep, to goat - any animal milk you can think of - cheese can be made from it. After all, in the end, despite the huge range of tastes and texture - salty to sweet, creamy to crumbly - the base of our addiction is one thing: proceessed milk curds. If you're vegan though, don't turn back! This website will cover vegan cheeses as well (though nutrition will differ from non-vegan varieties). But, despite being made from the same base, the magic of this food comes from the people (and the diffrent ingredients and methods they came up with). Different cultures came up with different methods of processing milk, and that gave birth to the wide variety that we see in the deli today! There's Cheddar from America, Parmesan from Italy, Gouda from the Netherlands, Halloumi from Cyprus. When you eat cheese, you're getting a taste of the different cultures of the globe!

Health Benefits

With the huge variety of cheese to try, it’s unsurprising that the opinion on it is divided. Yes, it can be high in saturated fats and sodium. (Unhealthy!) But yes, you can choose varieties that are low fat, and low sodium (healthy!).But, despite this division because of fat and sodium content, cheese does contain a good amount of nutrients to our body’s benefit.

Nutrients

  • Calcium: Calcium is good for building and maintaining your body’s bones.
  • Protein: Protein is necessary in muscles maintenance and building.
  • Magnesium: Great for bones and heart, magnesium is considered one of the body’s seven essential macronutrients.
  • Zinc: Supports your immune system.
  • Vitamin A: Helps with your immune system, vision, and your growth and development.
  • Vitamin D: Helps with bone and teeth development, and lowered risk of despression
  • Vitamin K: Aids with blood clotting to prevent excess bleeding.
